## PART 1: LOGIN PAGE (0:30 - 3:00)

### Accessing the Login Page

When you first visit the FIS Dashboard, you'll see a beautiful, professional login page with an animated background. The page features the FIS logo prominently displayed, and it's designed to be both welcoming and secure.

This login page is your secure entry point to the entire dashboard. Everything starts here.

### Microsoft Authentication

The dashboard uses Microsoft authentication for secure access. This means you'll sign in using your Microsoft account credentials - the same account you might use for Office 365, Outlook, or other Microsoft services.

To sign in, simply click the blue "Sign in with Microsoft" button. This will redirect you to Microsoft's secure authentication page, where you'll enter your Microsoft account email and password.

Here's what makes this secure: Your credentials are handled entirely by Microsoft, not by our system. This integration ensures that your login is protected by Microsoft's enterprise-grade security. Plus, if you're already signed into Microsoft on your device, the process is seamless - you might just need to confirm your identity.

Another great benefit: When you sign in with Microsoft, the system automatically pulls your profile information - your name, email address, and even your job title - directly from your Microsoft account. This means you don't have to manually enter this information, and it's always up to date.

### Access Request Flow

Now, if your account isn't yet authorized to access the dashboard, you'll see a helpful message after signing in. The system will display an "Access Required" notification that shows your email address and explains that you need administrator approval.

Don't worry - this is simple to resolve. Just click the "Request Access" button. When you do, you'll see a confirmation message letting you know that your request has been sent successfully.

Here's what happens next: The administrator receives a notification about your access request. They'll review it and approve your access. Once approved, you'll receive a notification, and you'll be able to sign in and access the dashboard immediately.

This access control system ensures that only authorized personnel can view your sensitive workroom performance data.

### Successful Login

Once you're successfully logged in, you'll be automatically redirected to the main dashboard - what we call the Performance Operational Dashboard, or POD for short.

In the top right corner of the screen, you'll see your profile information. This includes your name and your job title, which was automatically pulled from your Microsoft account. 

Now, here's something important: you can click on your profile to access your settings, where you'll be able to choose your workroom and your position. This helps the system personalize your dashboard experience and show you the most relevant data for your role and location. You can update these settings anytime by clicking on your profile and navigating to the settings page.

You can also use your profile menu to sign out when you're done.

Congratulations - you're now in the dashboard and ready to explore!

## PART 2: VISUAL DATA - PERFORMANCE OPERATIONAL DASHBOARD (3:00 - 12:00)

### Understanding the POD

The Performance Operational Dashboard - or POD - is the heart of the FIS Dashboard. This is where you'll see comprehensive visual analytics of your workroom performance data, all in one place.

The POD displays several key components: a heatmap visualization with color-coded workroom performance, key performance metrics at a glance, a geographic map showing workroom locations, and detailed data tables with rankings and breakdowns.

Everything you need to understand your workroom performance is right here on this page.

### The Heatmap - Your Primary Tool

The heatmap is the primary visualization tool on the POD. Each workroom is displayed as a card with color-coded performance indicators that make it easy to see at a glance which workrooms are performing well and which need attention.

Let me break down what you'll see on each workroom card. First, you'll see the workroom name. Then, there's the WPI Score - that's the Workroom Performance Index, which is a weighted score that gives you an overall performance rating.

Each card also shows a performance label - either "Top Performing," "Moderate," or "Critical." This label is based on the WPI score.

You'll also see key metrics displayed: LTR performance, Labor PO dollar amounts, Vendor Debits, and Store Mix information. Plus, if there are any operational or financial risks, those are clearly indicated.

Now, let's talk about the color coding system, because this is really important for quick visual understanding. Green cards indicate Top Performing workrooms - these have a WPI score of 85 or higher. Yellow cards show Moderate performance - these workrooms have a WPI score between 70 and 84. And red cards indicate Critical workrooms that need immediate attention - these have a WPI score below 70.

This visual system makes it incredibly easy to quickly scan the dashboard and identify which workrooms are excelling and which ones need support.

### Issues to Fix - Taking Action

Now, here's a really important feature: Many workroom cards will show an "Issues to Fix" link at the bottom. You'll see text like "1 issue to fix" or "2 issues to fix" displayed in blue, underlined text.

These issues are automatically identified by the system based on performance metrics. For example, you might see issues related to high cycle times, high reschedule rates, vendor debit exposure, or missing data - indicated by warning triangles next to specific metrics.

When you click on "Issues to Fix," a detailed dialog box opens up showing you exactly what needs to be addressed for that workroom. This dialog provides specific, actionable recommendations called "Fix This Now" items.

These recommendations are tailored to the specific problems identified. For instance, if there's a high reschedule rate, you'll see recommendations like "Confirm installer availability before you promise a date" or "Keep the original install date by pivoting to an alternate installer when needed."

If there are vendor debit issues, you'll see recommendations about reducing vendor debit exposure and scheduling installs with installers who have strong LTR performance.

If there are cycle time problems, you'll get specific guidance on improving scheduling, capacity planning, and work order management.

Each recommendation is practical and actionable - they're not just telling you what's wrong, they're telling you exactly how to fix it. This makes the dashboard not just a reporting tool, but a true action-oriented platform that helps you improve performance.

You can review all the issues, take notes, and then close the dialog to return to the heatmap. This feature transforms the dashboard from a passive reporting system into an active tool for continuous improvement.

### Understanding the WPI Score

The Workroom Performance Index, or WPI, is a comprehensive weighted score calculated from multiple performance factors. Let me explain how it's calculated, because understanding this will help you interpret what you're seeing.

Fifty percent of the WPI score comes from LTR Performance - that's your Labor-to-Revenue ratio. This is a critical metric because it shows how efficiently labor is being used relative to sales.

Five percent comes from Details Cycle Time - this measures how long it takes to complete detail work.

Thirteen percent is based on Job Cycle Time - the time it takes to complete jobs.

Fourteen percent comes from Work Order Cycle Time - the total time it takes to process a work order from start to finish.

Eight percent is based on Reschedule Rate - how frequently work needs to be rescheduled.

And finally, ten percent comes from Vendor Debits - your vendor debit exposure.

This comprehensive scoring system gives you a complete, balanced picture of workroom performance. It's not just about one metric - it considers multiple factors that all contribute to overall success.

### Top Performing Workrooms Table

Below the heatmap, you'll find the "Top Performing Workrooms" table. This shows the top 15 workrooms ranked by their WPI score, so you can see your best performers at a glance.

As you scroll through this table, you'll see several columns of information. There's the rank - showing each workroom's position in the performance ranking. The workroom name. The WPI Score, displayed with color-coded badges that match the heatmap colors - green for top performers, yellow for moderate, and red for critical.

You'll also see LTR Performance - the Labor-to-Revenue percentage. Labor PO dollar amounts - showing the total labor purchase order volume. Vendor Debit Exposure - showing vendor debit amounts. And Store Mix - the number of stores each workroom serves.

This table is incredibly useful for identifying your best performers and understanding what makes them successful. You can use this information to identify best practices and apply them to other workrooms.

### Workrooms Most Responsible for Moving Your Business

On the right side of the POD, you'll see a section called "Workrooms Most Responsible for Moving Your Business." This shows the top four workrooms based on their WPI score - these are the workrooms that are truly driving your business forward.

The section features a colorful pie chart that visualizes the WPI score distribution among these top performers. Each slice of the pie represents one workroom, and you can see the workroom name, the WPI score, and the percentage contribution right on the chart.

Below the pie chart, there's a detailed table that provides more information. It shows the workroom name, the WPI Score with color-coded badges, and the number of records. This helps you understand not just which workrooms are performing well, but also how much data is behind those scores.

This section is particularly valuable for leadership, as it clearly shows which workrooms are having the biggest impact on overall business performance.

### The Geographic Map

At the bottom of the POD, you'll find a geographic map showing all your workroom locations. This is a powerful visual tool that helps you understand performance by location.

Each workroom is marked with a colored pin that corresponds to its performance level. Green pins indicate Top Performing workrooms. Yellow pins show Moderate performance. And red pins mark Critical workrooms that need attention.

When you click on any marker, a popup appears with detailed information about that workroom. You'll see the workroom name, the WPI Score, the performance label, Sales information in the form of Labor PO dollars, Total Cost, and Store Mix count.

This geographic view is incredibly valuable because it helps you identify regional patterns. Maybe you'll notice that workrooms in a particular area are all performing well, or perhaps you'll see a cluster of workrooms that need support. This spatial understanding can inform strategic decisions about resource allocation and support.

### Additional Analytics

The POD also includes several additional analytics that provide even more insights.

There's a "Workroom Performance Index by Workroom" section that includes both a bar chart and a detailed table. The bar chart shows WPI scores across all workrooms in a visual format, making it easy to compare performance levels.

There's also an "Average Labor PO Dollar by Workroom" bar chart that shows labor volume by workroom, helping you understand where your labor investment is going.

And there's a comprehensive WPI table that provides a complete breakdown with Labor PO dollars, Vendor Debits, and more detailed metrics.

What's really important to understand is that all of these visualizations use the same data source as the heatmap. This ensures consistency across the entire dashboard - you're always looking at the same underlying data, just presented in different ways to help you understand different aspects of performance.

### Notifications - Stay Informed and Take Action

One of the most powerful features of the dashboard is the notification system. You'll see a bell icon in the top right corner of the screen, next to your profile. This is your notification center.

When there are new notifications, you'll see a red badge with a number indicating how many unread notifications you have. This helps you stay on top of important issues that need your attention.

Notifications are automatically generated by the system when performance issues are detected. For example, you might receive a notification if a workroom's LTR score drops below 85 percent, or if the reschedule rate exceeds 20 percent, or if cycle times are too high, or if vendor debit exposure becomes critical.

Each notification is specific to a workroom, so you know exactly which location needs attention. The notification message clearly describes what the issue is and why it matters.

Here's where it gets really powerful: Many notifications include a "Fix it Now" button. When you click this button, the system automatically takes you to the appropriate corrective action form, pre-filled with the workroom information.

For instance, if you get a notification about a high reschedule rate for a specific workroom, clicking "Fix it Now" will take you directly to the Reschedule Rate Accountability Report form, with that workroom already selected. You can immediately start documenting the issue and your corrective actions.

If it's an LTR performance issue, clicking "Fix it Now" takes you to the LTR Workroom Performance Report form. For cycle time issues, you'll be taken to the Work Cycle Time Corrective Report. For vendor debit issues, you'll go to the Vendor Debit Accountability Report.

This seamless flow from notification to action form makes it incredibly easy to address issues quickly. You don't have to remember which form to use or manually select the workroom - the system guides you through the entire process.

You can also click directly on a notification to mark it as read. If you want to clear all notifications at once, there's a "Mark All as Read" option. And you can delete individual notifications if you no longer need them.

The notification system ensures that important issues don't get overlooked. It's your proactive alert system that helps you maintain high performance standards across all workrooms.

### Filtering and Navigation

On the left side of the screen, you'll find the sidebar with navigation and filtering options. This is your control center for navigating the dashboard.

You can filter by workroom using the dropdown menu at the top of the sidebar. This is really useful when you want to focus on a specific workroom and see all its data in detail.

The sidebar also provides navigation to other important pages. You can access Survey Misc to view survey data, Analytics for deeper insights, Store Overview for store-level analytics, and more.

If you're an administrator, you'll also see options to export data as PDF reports and to upload new T1/T2 scorecard files. These functions are clearly marked and easy to use.

The sidebar is designed to be user-friendly. On desktop, it's always visible and can be collapsed if you need more screen space. On mobile devices, there's a hamburger menu icon that opens and closes the sidebar, ensuring the dashboard works great on any device.

## PART 3: SURVEY DATA (12:00 - 18:00)

### Understanding Survey Data

Survey data is a crucial component of the FIS Dashboard. It provides valuable customer feedback and installer performance metrics that complement the operational data you see in the POD.

To access survey data, simply click on "Survey Misc" in the sidebar navigation. This will take you to a dedicated page where all survey-related information is organized and displayed.

Survey data includes several important types of information. There are LTR Scores - that's Likelihood to Recommend ratings from customers. Craft Scores - ratings of craftsmanship quality. Professional Scores - ratings of professionalism. Survey Comments - actual written feedback from customers. Installer Information - including installer names and their individual performance metrics. And Company Data - company information gathered from surveys.

This survey data is incredibly valuable because it gives you the customer's perspective on your work. It's qualitative feedback that helps explain the quantitative metrics you see in the POD.

### Survey Data Upload

Survey data is uploaded separately from visual data. This is an important design feature because it allows you to update survey information independently of operational data. You might get new survey data more frequently than you update your operational scorecards, so this separation gives you flexibility.

As an administrator, you'll see two distinct upload sections in the sidebar. One is for Visual Data Upload - that's for your T1/T2 scorecard operational data. The other is for Survey Data Upload - that's specifically for survey and customer feedback data.

To upload survey data, simply click "Choose Survey Data File" and select your survey Excel file from your computer. The system will then process the file, which you can watch happen through a progress indicator.

During the upload process, the system parses the survey data, extracts LTR scores, Craft scores, and Professional scores. It pulls out installer names and company information. And it stores all this data separately from your visual data, maintaining clean data separation.

The upload process is designed to be straightforward and user-friendly, with clear progress indicators so you always know what's happening.

### Survey Data Integration with Heatmap

One of the most powerful features of the dashboard is how survey data integrates with the heatmap on the POD. This integration makes your performance metrics more accurate and meaningful.

When survey data is available, the heatmap automatically uses survey LTR scores instead of calculated LTR percentages. This is important because survey-based scores come directly from customer feedback, making them more accurate and reliable than calculated values.

You'll see this reflected in the workroom cards on the heatmap. The LTR Performance rating will be based on actual survey data, and it will be labeled accordingly. Excellent means an LTR score greater than 9.0. Good is between 8.0 and 9.0. Moderate is between 7.0 and 8.0. Poor is between 6.0 and 7.0. And Critical is below 6.0.

This integration means that the WPI scores you see on the heatmap are influenced by real customer feedback, not just operational metrics. This gives you a more complete and accurate picture of workroom performance.

### Survey Misc Page Features

The Survey Misc page provides detailed survey analytics organized into several helpful sections.

First, there's the Survey Scores Overview. This section shows you average LTR, Craft, and Professional scores across all your surveys. You can see score distributions and trends over time, helping you understand overall customer satisfaction levels.

Second, there's Installer Performance. This section shows installer names and their individual scores, performance rankings, and detailed metrics for each installer. This is incredibly valuable for understanding which installers are excelling and which might need additional training or support.

Third, there's Company Information. This section displays company names from surveys, company performance metrics, and company-specific analytics. This helps you understand how different companies rate your services.

And fourth, there's Customer Comments. This is a searchable database of all survey comments and feedback. You can filter comments by workroom or installer, making it easy to find specific feedback or identify patterns in customer comments.

Each of these sections is designed to give you actionable insights from your survey data.

### Survey Data Tables and Charts

The Survey Misc page includes comprehensive tables and charts that help you visualize and understand your survey data.

There are Score Distribution Charts that provide visual representations of how scores are distributed. These charts make it easy to see patterns - are most scores clustered in a particular range? Are there outliers that need attention?

There's an Installer Performance Table that provides detailed installer rankings. This table shows you which installers are performing best and which might need support.

There's a Workroom Survey Summary that aggregates survey data by workroom, so you can see how each workroom is performing from a customer perspective.

And there's Time-based Analysis that shows survey trends over time. This helps you understand if customer satisfaction is improving, declining, or staying stable.

You can filter all of this data in multiple ways. Filter by workroom to focus on specific locations. Filter by installer name to see individual performance. Filter by date range to look at specific time periods. Filter by score ranges to identify high or low performers. Or filter by company to see company-specific insights.

This flexibility makes the Survey Misc page a powerful tool for deep-dive analysis.

### Raw Survey Data

The dashboard also stores and displays raw survey data values exactly as they appear in your Excel files. This is important because sometimes you need to see the original data, not just aggregated or processed versions.

The system preserves Raw Column L Values - these are the original survey responses. Raw Craft Scores - the original craft ratings. Raw Professional Scores - the original professional ratings. Raw Labor Categories - labor category classifications. Raw Company Values - company information. And Raw Installer Names - installer name data.

This raw data is always available for detailed analysis, ensuring you never lose access to the original information from your surveys.

### Survey Data Best Practices

Let me share some best practices for working with survey data that will help you get the most value from this feature.

First, upload survey data regularly to keep your metrics current. Fresh data means accurate insights.

Second, ensure your Excel files follow the correct format. The system is designed to work with specific file structures, so following the format ensures smooth uploads and accurate data processing.

Third, remember that survey data and visual data can be uploaded independently. This gives you flexibility to update one without affecting the other.

Fourth, always review how survey data affects heatmap scores after uploading. This integration is powerful, and understanding how it works will help you interpret the POD more accurately.

And fifth, use the filtering options liberally. The ability to focus on specific workrooms, installers, or time periods makes the data much more actionable.

Following these practices will help you maintain high-quality survey data and get maximum value from the analytics.
