## UPLOADING DATA (1:30 - 2:30)

**[SCREENSHOT: File upload section]**

"Now, let's upload your data. The dashboard supports two types of data uploads:

First, **Visual Data** - This is your main workroom performance data from Excel files. Click 'Upload Visual Data' and select your Excel file. The system automatically parses columns like workroom name, store number, sales, labor PO, vendor debits, and more.

Second, **Survey Data** - Upload survey responses that include customer feedback, LTR scores, craft scores, and professional scores.

The system intelligently merges both datasets, matching records by store number and workroom name. Once uploaded, your data is securely stored in the cloud database and immediately available across all dashboard views."

**Fix This Now** - Actionable recommendations for workrooms needing improvement. Green checkmark means no critical issues.

## WORKROOM PERFORMANCE INDEX (5:00 - 5:30)

**[SCREENSHOT: WPI section]**

"The Workroom Performance Index, or WPI, is a weighted score that combines:
- 50% from Labor PO performance
- 30% from Labor PO dollar volume
- 20% from Vendor Debit discipline

## TOP PERFORMING WORKROOMS - DETAILED TUTORIAL (5:30 - 7:00)

**[SCREENSHOT: Top Performing Workrooms table]**

"Let's dive into the **Top Performing Workrooms** section. This is one of the most important analytics tools in the dashboard.

**[SCREENSHOT: Table header]**

"This table ranks all your workrooms based on their **Workroom Performance Index**, or WPI score. The workroom with the highest WPI score is ranked number one, and so on.

**[SCREENSHOT: Column headers]**

"Each row shows seven key metrics:

**Rank** - The workroom's position from best to worst performer. Number one is your top performer.

**Workroom** - The name of the workroom location, like Tampa, Sarasota, or Naples.

**Stores** - How many different stores this workroom serves. More stores often means better coverage and distribution.

**LTR%** - Labor-to-Revenue percentage. This shows what percentage of sales goes to labor costs. Lower is better - you want this number to be as low as possible. Green badges indicate excellent performance, yellow means moderate, and red means high labor costs relative to revenue.

**Labor PO $** - The total dollar amount spent on labor for this workroom. This is your labor cost.

**Vendor Debits** - Charges from vendors. Notice that negative numbers are actually good - those are vendor credits or refunds! Green negative numbers mean you're getting money back.

**WPI Score** - The Workroom Performance Index, a weighted score from 0 to 100. Higher is better. This single number combines multiple factors to give you an overall performance rating.

**[SCREENSHOT: Top ranked workrooms]**

"Looking at the top performers, you can see:
- Workrooms with high WPI scores, often 80 or above
- Low LTR percentages, meaning efficient labor usage
- Good store coverage
- Favorable vendor debit situations

**[SCREENSHOT: Color coding]**

"Notice the color coding:
- **Green badges** indicate excellent performance
- **Yellow badges** show moderate performance
- **Red or orange badges** highlight areas needing attention

**[SCREENSHOT: Scrolling through table]**

"You can scroll through the entire table to see all workrooms ranked. This helps you:
- Identify your best performers to learn from them
- Spot underperformers that need support
- Compare workrooms side-by-side

Use this table to understand what makes your top performers successful and apply those lessons to other workrooms."

## OPERATIONAL METRICS - DETAILED TUTORIAL (8:30 - 10:30)

**[SCREENSHOT: Operational Metrics dashboard]**

"Now let's explore the **Operational Metrics** dashboard. This section provides real-time KPIs that give you a pulse on your business operations.

**[SCREENSHOT: Animated counters]**

"You'll notice these numbers animate and count up when the page loads - this creates a dynamic, engaging view of your metrics.

**[SCREENSHOT: Jobs Completed]**

"**Jobs Completed** - This shows the total number of jobs that have been finished across all workrooms. This is calculated directly from your uploaded data - each record represents a completed job. Watch this number grow as you upload more data.

**[SCREENSHOT: Jobs Pending]**

"**Jobs Pending** - This is an estimated number of jobs currently in progress or waiting to be completed. The system calculates this as approximately 12% of your total jobs, which is a typical industry average for work-in-progress inventory.

**[SCREENSHOT: Return/Redo Jobs]**

"**Return and Redo Jobs** - This shows estimated jobs that need to be redone or have returns. Calculated as about 4% of total jobs, this helps you understand quality issues and customer satisfaction challenges.

**[SCREENSHOT: Installation Quality Score]**

"**Installation Quality Score** - This is a calculated metric based on your cycle times. The system analyzes how long jobs take to complete. Faster completion times indicate better quality and efficiency. The score ranges from 75 to 95, with higher being better. It's calculated by looking at average cycle times - shorter cycles mean higher quality scores.

**[SCREENSHOT: Customer Satisfaction Score]**

"**Customer Satisfaction Score** - This metric is derived from your margin rates and overall performance. Higher margins and better performance metrics translate to higher customer satisfaction. The system analyzes your financial performance to estimate how satisfied your customers are with the service quality.

**[SCREENSHOT: Average Labor Hours]**

"**Average Labor Hours** - This shows the typical number of labor hours per job across all workrooms. It's calculated from your Labor PO data, giving you insight into how much time is invested in each job on average.

**[SCREENSHOT: On-Time Completion Rate]**

"**On-Time Completion Rate** - This percentage shows how many jobs are completed within the target timeframe. The system compares your actual cycle times against a 30-day target. Jobs completed in 30 days or less count as on-time. Higher percentages mean better operational efficiency.

**[SCREENSHOT: Real-time updates]**

"One of the best features - all these metrics update in **real-time**. When you upload new data:
- Jobs Completed increases
- All calculated metrics automatically recalculate
- The animated counters refresh with new numbers
- No page reload needed

**[SCREENSHOT: Using the metrics]**

"These operational metrics help you:
- **Track productivity** - See how many jobs you're completing
- **Monitor quality** - Installation quality and customer satisfaction scores
- **Identify bottlenecks** - High pending jobs might indicate capacity issues
- **Measure efficiency** - On-time completion rates show operational effectiveness
- **Plan resources** - Average labor hours help with staffing decisions

**[SCREENSHOT: Dashboard overview]**

"Together, these seven metrics give you a comprehensive view of your operational health. Green numbers indicate good performance, while yellow or red might signal areas needing attention.

Use this dashboard regularly to track trends and make data-driven operational decisions."
